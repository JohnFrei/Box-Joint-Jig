Checking the print scale
Measure the space between intersections of the diagonal grid, two apart. This should be exactly 20 cm. If it is slightly more or less, your printer, especially if it is a laser printer, is introducing some scaling. You can scale the PDF from acrobat reader for printing. For example, if you measure 19.8 cm instead of 20.0 cm, then calculate as follows:

20.0 / 19.8 = 1.0101 = 101%

Then enter that number for the print scale. In the above example, 101%.

Laser printers sometimes have some scale errors as much as 1%. Ink jet printers are much better at keeping printouts precisely to scale.

If you have access to a printer that can print on larger sheets of paper such as 11x17, A3, or a large format printer, you can click on any of the images below and open it in a graphics program of your choice and print it at 127 DPI (50 dots per centimeter). You can also use the free eval version of my BigPrint program to print these (Windows only). Just download the free eval of BigPrint, run it, then drag any of the images into BigPrint to open it.

Gear templates

The best way to print gear templates of the correct size is to print selected pages of all_sizes.pdf.
After printing, measure the printout to make sure the grid has 1 cm spacing. Larger gears are spread over two pages.

If you are using internet explorer or Firefox, you can also use the HTML files below, printing at exactly 100%.
check the grid spacing after printing. You can also use an image editing program of your choice to print the images.
Click on the right column to get the bitmap, and print it to the size specified. All images are at a resolution of 4 pixels per millimeter (101.6 DPI)

Finally, you can also use the online gear template generator to make the gear shapes.
Use a tooth spacing of 16.8 mm, contact angle of 8 degrees. Be sure to calibrate the scale.
